
# Employee Attrition Analysis Using Python

##  Project Overview
This project analyzes the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset to understand the factors influencing employee turnover. The analysis includes data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and visualization to identify patterns and generate actionable insights.

##  Objectives
- Analyze employee demographics and work-related factors.
- Identify the key drivers of employee attrition.
- Discover patterns affecting employee retention.
- Generate insights to support HR decision-making.

##  Dataset Information
- **Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
- **Records:** 1,470
- **Features:** 35
- **Target Variable:** Attrition

##  Tools and Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

##  Data Cleaning
- Verified dataset structure and data types.
- Checked for missing values.
- Checked duplicate records.
- Ensured data consistency.
- No major data cleaning was required.

##  Exploratory Data Analysis
The following analyses were performed:
- Attrition Distribution
- Gender Distribution
- Department-wise Employee Count
- Age Distribution
- Monthly Income Distribution
- Attrition by Gender
- Overtime vs Attrition
- Monthly Income vs Attrition
- Years at Company vs Attrition
- Correlation Heatmap

##  Key Insights
- Employees working overtime are more likely to leave the company.
- Lower monthly income is associated with higher attrition.
- Employees with fewer years at the company have higher turnover.
- Job satisfaction significantly impacts employee retention.
- Research & Development has the highest number of employees.
- Most employees belong to the 30–40 age group.

##  Conclusion
The analysis revealed that overtime, monthly income, years at the company, and job satisfaction are major factors influencing employee attrition. These insights can help organizations develop effective employee retention strategies.
